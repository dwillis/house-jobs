MEM-193-26
Under the supervision of the office’s Legislative Counsel, the Law Clerk will support the
Congresswoman’s legislative and oversight work by conducting legal and policy research,
preparing for and staffing Judiciary Committee hearings and markups, developing vote
recommendations, and helping to advance legislation. Law Clerks will draft legal
memoranda, hearing scripts, policy memos, briefing materials, and other written products.
This position is ideal for high-achieving law students, preferably 2Ls or 3Ls, with a strong
interest in public law, policymaking, politics, congressional oversight, or government
service. Law Clerks will have the opportunity to engage in serious, substantive work across
a wide range of issue areas, including antitrust, administrative law, civil rights and civil
liberties, criminal justice, government reform (i.e., anti-corruption), intellectual property,
oversight, privacy, technology (e.g., artificial intelligence), and voting rights.
The clerkship is unpaid but may be completed for academic credit. Women, people of
color, LGBTQ+ individuals, new Americans, people with disabilities, and members of other
underrepresented communities are strongly encouraged to apply.
The application period for Fall 2026 Law Clerks is now open and will close on July 9th at
11:59 PM ET.
To apply, please email the following materials to
Jayapal.DCLawclerk@mail.house.gov: (1) resume, (2) cover letter, (3) list of three
references with email addresses, and (4) unofficial undergraduate and graduate
transcripts. Please combine all application materials into a single PDF and use the
subject line: “Law Clerk – FirstName LastName.”
